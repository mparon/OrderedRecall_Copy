Welcome!

You have been invited to take part in a research study being conducted by the Computational Memory Lab at the University of Pennsylvania. In this study, you will see or hear lists of words, and later be asked to recall them. Full instructions will be provided before the study, and you will be given the opportunity to practice the task before the main phase of the experiment begins.

The experiment will last approximately 30-50 minutes, not counting breaks. The only minor risk of the study is fatigue from completing the task. There will be opportunities to rest between trials, in case you need to take a break at any point. You will be given up to two and a half hours to complete the study before the HIT expires. You must participate throughout all trials of the experiment (i.e., answering all questions, making recalls when prompted) for us to mark the HIT as complete. We will mark HITs as complete within two business days.

Your participation is completely voluntary, and you may stop the experiment at any time. If you have any questions about your rights as a research participant, or about this study in general, please contact the principal investigator:
